I could certainly start keeping track of that information, but unfortunately I've never done so in the past, so I can't tell you what the trends have been up to this point.

Of course, the trends will depend on what games we typically play. For example, in the past we played a lot of Pai Gow Poker, so I'd guess that's where a great deal of our jackpots were won.

Since we now mostly play Hold 'Em and Omaha, I think it's a safe guess that most of our jackpots these days are won in Omaha.

It might also be interesting to see what percentage of the time jackpot is won in a particular game. For example, "Out of the x number of times we played Omaha, jackpot was won y times." (I bet if we played more 7-27, the percentage would be huge; I think that game provides the easiest way to win it.) But you know what? I'm not going to keep track of how many times we play a certain game. I'd like to continue to enjoy Card Night. Forget I said anything.

However, writing down what game was played when jackpot was won is no problem.

On a related topic, I would guess that between 5% and 10% of jackpots are won with a straight flush. And in the history of Card Night (going back to before we even kept stats), there have been only two or three jackpots won with a royal flush. Also, Paul once won 100% of jackpot by getting five kings in Bloody Boy.
