Pai Gow or Hold 'Em: Where is jackpot more likely to occur?

From: Mark D

October 3, 2005, 4:53 p.m.

So, the question is, "Are you more likely to win jackpot in Pai Gow Poker or in Texas Hold 'em?"

My initial take on it was, "They're both the same. In both cases you're dealt seven random cards."

But the argument was made that they're not the same because they're dealt differently. In Pai Gow, everyone gets seven unique cards. In Hold 'em, there are five community cards.

Could the community cards make it easier to win jackpot?

I wrote a program to find out. (The program just looked for the four-of-a-kind jackpot payout to make it easy; in other words, I didn't look for straight flushes.) The program dealt 1 million rounds of Pai Gow to seven players, for a total of 7 million hands. Then the program dealt 1 million rounds of Hold 'Em to seven players, again for a total of 7 million hands.

Results: For both Pai Gow and Hold 'Em, a player can expect to get four-of-a-kind once in every 595 hands. They're exactly the same.

HOWEVER, keep in mind that with Pai Gow there's no folding, but with Hold 'Em, there is. So, you could argue that because people could end up folding a jackpot hand in Hold 'Em but not in Pai Gow, jackpot will be won in Pai Gow more often.

MDV

P.S. At Card Night, with seven players, if every player stays in every time, we should see four of a kind once in every 85 rounds of Hold 'Em. (595 / 7 = 85)
