Info from discussions at yesterday's Padres game

From: Mark DeVol

April 11, 2001, 9:56 a.m.

  • Charlton Heston is 77 years old (younger than anyone at the game thought).

  • Paul made an interesting claim. As baseball fans know, right-handed batters do better against left-handed pitchers than against right-handed pitchers. And, conversely, left-handed batters do better against RHPs than against LHPs.

    Paul suggested that since there are more RHPs than LHPs in the major leagues, right-handed batters do better against RHPs than left-handed batters do against LHPs, simply because the batters see more RHPs. (The ratio of RHPs to LHPs is about 8 to 3.)

    Well, it turns out that the statistics back up Paul's claim. According to Scoresheet Fantasy Sports, right-handed batters generally hit about 20 points lower against RHPs than against LHPs, whereas left-handed batters hit about 35 points lower against LHPs than against RHPs.
