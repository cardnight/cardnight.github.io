Letter from Mark DeVol: Coyne, change the way you play.

November 14, 2000

Look, Mike Coyne. In all seriousness, the reason you're losing so much is because you're BETTING so much. Instead of throwing ten dollars out there on a single Pai Gow hand, just bet a couple. Same with the sides: If you must bet the sides (and I know you must), just throw out a buck or two instead of five.

Look at the way I play. I haven't gotten the nickname "No Action" for nothing. I consistently bet just two dollars when someone is dealing a bank game (Pai Gow, Nines Up, etc.). Also, I very rarely go for more than three dollars in Acey-Deucy. Now, "No Money" and "Turtle Boy" might call me wimpy (and if Stan were still around, he'd probably say, "You idiot!"), but this wimpy idiot is laughing all the way to the bank.

Bottom line: Don't go for the big hit. Don't try to win everything back in the last half hour. Just chip away little by little. That way if you do lose, you don't lose big.

Early retirement? Would you like a blouse to go with that skirt?

Mark "The Genius" DeVol
