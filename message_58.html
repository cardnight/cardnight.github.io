Letter from Mike Coyne: Destroying Paul? Hmmmm

December 15, 2000

Well, maybe my tactics were all towards the wrong person after all. I think that I will change the way I bet and be more like Warren "No Guts" Huyser and only stay in a game when I have a SURE win. Hmmm, maybe? I think that being sucked into a game hoping for just one card to make my hand is not the way to play the game. As everyone is well aware, the Warren man only goes against or goes in when he basically has a great hand, never going in on marginal hands and being a gambler. I am sort of thinking that it is the way to start playing. How many nights has he ended up being a loser with that strategy? Not very often I must say.

I think that times are changing for me, and also my card playing must change as well.

I am not going to let Paul "M&M" Huyser get the best of me anymore. I am not going to play his stupid little Pai Paul Gow game with any REAL money from now on. I think that it is very ridiculous that he plays that game with (oh never mind, you all know)!

I also don't know why he is against the Good Doctor and EVERYONE'S favorite uncle, Uncle Ike Huyser, either. You'd think that having the same last name they would have some kinda bond between them, don't you? Maybe Paul is just MAD that Uncle Ike is a better person than he is? I am sure that if Uncle Ike refurbished a wooden boat and said that it was going to be in the water on a certain day, I would tend to believe him. That is the nature of Ike, telling the TRUTH! How many times over and over again has Paul given us dates on when the boat will be in the water? WAY too many to count. I do hope someday that it goes in the water. And I know on that day, the sun will be shining and the birds will be singing and the air will smell of sea air, and I know exactly were I will be: 30 miles inland! There is NO WAY I will EVER be on that boat in the water!

In closing: Yes, I will be there on Monday Night Card Night at the DeVol's House playing a different kinda card game and enjoying the great shows on TV that we are accustomed to watching.

Michael Coyne
