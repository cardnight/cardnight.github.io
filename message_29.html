Letter from Paul Huyser: Odds for this Monday, et al.

November 9, 2000

Another night of cards is over. I didn't care for the outcome, but that's what happens sometimes in this unpredictable game of poker. Congratulations to the victors.

Proposal #1
The issue of the misdeal needs to addressed. We don't seem to have any set of rules on this matter, and our decisions too often look like NFL officiating. So please consider.

Proposal #2
No more jackpot. Too many ways to win, too many decks of cards. I propose we collect the money as usual, and when the pot reaches a desired amount, we hire a topless dealer for the night (female). And, no Mike S., it can't be you. Just a suggestion; tell me what you think.

This morning I read Coyne's article "Paul... thinking". Must say it has brightened my day. Very funny material. Let me quote a sentence from Coyne: "If I have a winning night is he gonna give me 30 dollars if I win per every dollar that I may win?" I don't know what Coyne means, but I like it.

I also read that you are quite fond of throwing up. If this is true, I suggest you do this: Scarf down a couple of stadium dogs and heave in Mike Stefanidis' car. Don't be a wimp. Not in a Dorrito's Bag, but on the seat, or down the heating vent. Either way would be acceptable. And the good thing about it is Mike S. won't even care. He'll just go home and puke himself.

One more thing, Coyne. What's the big deal with the soft drinks? Mark is kind enough to provide us with a wide range of popular cold beverages. So please for the sake of all of us stop whining about who likes what drinks. I realize it's an important subject to you, but you're killing me. If you are really trying to put me in my grave, I suggest writing another piece about what drinks you like and dislike. About two pages should be sufficient.

Mark DeVol, I know what you mean. Sometimes we must go to great lengths to cheer Mike S. up. Unfortunately this time it wasn't the case. As to your nickname Mark "The Genius" DeVol... a little over the top. I'm thinking more along the lines of Mark "No Action" DeVol. Not trying to start anything; just thought it had a better ring.

And now Paul's best friend:
